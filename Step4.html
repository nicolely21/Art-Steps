The last step to make your drawing pop out is to add some shading and lighting. Just the little things could make an image a lot better. For example, shading in some shadows. Choose an area of where your light source could be coming from. That means that the shadow of some furniture would be facing the other direction. You can decide how strong your light source is, when you make the shadow darker, it means that the light source is very bright, when your shadow is week (not so dark) it means that the light source isn’t as strong.



Also pay attention to when items are blocking other items, they could possibly be blocking the light from passing through making the whole area they’re in dark so add a shadow. Here is what it could look like.



As you can see, the light source is coming from the left, it could be from a window. Since the light source is coming from that direction, there should be a shadow. In my drawing, the shadow is facing the opposite direction from the light source to make it look more realistic.

Tips/advice:

always add a new layer for each step so you can go back to edit it! You can name it however you want, for step 4, I named it details.

Use a darker colour with very little opacity for shadowing

Use a light realistic colour for light, for example, white, yellow or blue. Make the opacity low.
